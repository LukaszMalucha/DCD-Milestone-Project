Machine Learning: Clustering

Overview:

Clustering is a main task of exploratory data mining that involves dividing the population or data points with similar characteristics and assigning them into clusters. Clustering is a unsupervised learning method and is a common technique for statistical data analysis. The output of the algorithm would be a set of “labels” assigning each point to one of the clusters.

Real life example:

  • Identifying groups of houses according to their characteristics.
  • Classification of plants and animals given their features.
  • Customer segmentation.
  • Classify the crimes based on age groups.
